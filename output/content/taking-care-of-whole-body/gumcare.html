Nutrition & Risk Factors

Good gum health starts with internal care. Nutritional deficiencies can harm gum tissue, and unhealthy habits can speed up bone loss.

The Gum Health Diet

Vitamin C

Necessary for the synthesis of collagen. Insufficient levels can lead to weakened and bleeding gums (similar to scurvy).
Eat: Citrus, Bell Peppers, Kiwi.

ü•¨

Vitamin K

Crucial for blood clotting. Helps reduce bleeding during cleaning.
Eat: Spinach, Kale, Leafy Greens.

üíß

Hydration

Saliva acts as a natural defense against bacteria, but dehydration can cause dry mouth, leading to higher plaque acidity.
Action: Drink water after every meal.

The Danger Zone

Smoking / Vaping

The top risk factor: Nicotine constricts blood vessels, hiding signs of bleeding and speeding up bone decay.

üç¨

Sugar & Starch

Sugar nourishes the particular bacteria (Streptococcus mutans) responsible for producing the acid and toxins that lead to gingivitis.

Bruxism (Grinding)

Too much pressure on teeth can harm the periodontal ligament and bone, exacerbating gum disease that is already present.
